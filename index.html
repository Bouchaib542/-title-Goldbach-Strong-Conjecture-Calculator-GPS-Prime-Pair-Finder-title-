Probabilistic Justification of the GPS-Goldbach Model

Bahbouhi Bouchaib, Independent Researcher, Nantes, France

Abstract

We present a probabilistic justification for the GPS-Goldbach model based on a mathematical expression of the prime gap centered around E/2. Our goal is to explain why the pair (p, q) = (E/2 − δ, E/2 + δ) with both p and q prime becomes increasingly predictable as E grows. We compare this with known theorems and propose a new effective equation for locating Goldbach pairs.

1. Introduction

The Goldbach Conjecture asserts that every even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two primes. Numerous numerical verifications have confirmed its validity up to very large numbers. Our approach aims to provide a new probabilistic explanation based on an efficient predictive interval centered around E/2 and defined by:

δ(E) ≈ √E · (log log E) / log E

We call this the "GPS gap", and it functions as a predictive radius within which we expect to find both prime numbers (p, q) such that p + q = E. The remarkable efficiency of this interval is that it often contains both primes simultaneously.

2. Comparison to Known Theorems

Our GPS gap aligns closely with major probabilistic models such as Cramér’s model [1], the Prime Number Theorem [2], and bounds given in Hardy–Littlewood formulations [3]. However, most classical theorems provide only asymptotic density or statistical estimates, while our method suggests a practical and deterministic zone for locating prime pairs.

3. Main Result and Observations

For any even number E ≥ 10⁶, computing δ(E) gives an interval I = [E/2 − δ, E/2 + δ]. Empirically, this interval almost always contains two primes (p, q) satisfying p + q = E. This contrasts with classical bounds which do not guarantee proximity of both primes to E/2. Our model consistently yields these primes even for large E, demonstrating the effectiveness of the GPS radius.

4. Discussion and Implications

We interpret the GPS interval as a probabilistic well where the prime pair (p, q) “falls” into a symmetrical zone around E/2. This leads to the conjecture:

For every even number E ≥ 4, the interval I(E) = [E/2 − δ(E), E/2 + δ(E)] contains at least one pair (p, q) of primes such that E = p + q.

This can be viewed as a practical reformulation of the Strong Goldbach Conjecture, now expressed in a predictive, bounded form. We hope this brings new insights for future analytic or computational approaches.
